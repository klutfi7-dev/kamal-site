The Broken World

How structure fails before the first conversation

Walk into any MedTech conference in Europe and ask a commercial director how they plan to enter the GCC. The answer follows a predictable sequence: attend Arab Health, collect contacts, identify a distributor, get the registration, launch.

This is not a market entry strategy. It is a sequence of activities that feels like progress while the structural problems compound underneath.

A distributor is selected based on enthusiasm and portfolio coverage — not capability or market creation willingness. Exclusivity is granted. The registration goes in the distributor's name. Eighteen months later, nothing has been placed. The manufacturer wants to switch. They cannot — the registration is held by the distributor, and walking away means starting the regulatory clock from zero.

This is not an execution failure. It is an architecture failure. And it was determined in the first ninety days.

"The Gulf does not punish bad products. It punishes bad structure."

The Discipline

GCC Commercial Architecture

The structural discipline that converts a CE/FDA-cleared device into repeatable GCC revenue — before the wrong decisions are locked in. Not a strategy document. A system that operates.

Registration Ownership Architecture

Market-by-market registration structure ensuring the manufacturer — not the distributor — controls their market assets. SFDA, MOHAP, NHRA sequencing designed for switchability and control.

Distributor Selection & Activation

IPP-based partner identification, capability assessment, and structured activation. Distributor agreements with governance clauses, minimum commitments, and performance gates that protect your position.

KOL & Clinical Pull Strategy

KOL identification, relationship sequencing, and pilot program design. For category-creating products — building the clinical case before the sales conversation. Market pull that moves before the tender is written.

Commercial Narrative & Pricing

Distributor-facing business case, clinical positioning, and pricing architecture calibrated to GCC procurement realities — not assumptions carried from the home market.

Regulatory Sequencing

Entry market prioritisation, AR/LAR appointment, SFDA and MOHAP pathway management. Regulatory architecture designed to enter the right market first — not the easiest one.
